# R3 Project Resonance Review Methodology

## Question

How can K R&D Lab document a personal but rigorous project impression: what feels promising, what feels weak, what associations appear, and whether the project seems worth watching, supporting, testing, or avoiding?

## Scope

This is a separate method from a project roast.

A roast is a diagnostic stress-test with recommendations.

A resonance review is an argued perspective read. It captures perception, strategic taste, emotional signal, associations, doubts, and perceived future potential without forcing the output into a fix-it plan.

## What a resonance review is

A project resonance review asks:

- What does this project make me feel or think in the first encounter?
- What is interesting before any formal scoring starts?
- What feels authentic, useful, alive, overbuilt, vague, risky, or mispositioned?
- Which associations appear: market category, cultural pattern, governance risk, public-value signal, founder energy, interface mood?
- Do I personally see a future for this project?
- If yes, what kind of future: niche tool, civic experiment, venture, research artifact, community layer, media object, or infrastructure?
- If no, what blocks belief: unclear audience, weak proof, ethical risk, aesthetic mismatch, category confusion, or lack of urgency?

## Difference from a roast

| Format | Main job | Tone | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Project roast | Stress-test the project and produce improvements | sharp, constructive, diagnostic | blockers, scores, recommendations, action plan |
| Resonance review | Capture argued perception and future-belief signal | reflective, essay-like, analytical | impressions, associations, belief map, perspective verdict |

## Core lenses

| Lens | Main question | Typical output |
|---|---|---|
| First-contact signal | What is felt or understood first? | immediate impression, confusion points, curiosity hooks |
| Personal belief | Do I believe this can become something? | yes/no/maybe verdict with reasoning |
| Founder/project energy | What does the project seem to be trying to become? | energy profile, ambition level, seriousness signal |
| Category association | What does it remind me of? | adjacent products, cultural references, market analogies |
| Future shape | If it works, what could it become? | possible paths, not recommendations |
| Friction without prescription | What bothers me, but without turning it into a task list? | critical observations and open doubts |
| Public-value resonance | Does it feel socially meaningful or merely clever? | social relevance, risk, usefulness, cultural signal |

## Standard structure

1. Project snapshot
2. First impression
3. What I like
4. What I do not like or do not trust yet
5. Associations and category signals
6. Personal belief: do I see perspective?
7. What future I can imagine for the project
8. What still feels unresolved
9. Public-value or cultural meaning
10. Final perspective verdict

## Verdict vocabulary

Use one of these labels:

- `strong resonance` - the project feels meaningful, legible, and worth tracking
- `latent potential` - there is something real, but it is not visible enough yet
- `interesting but fragile` - the concept is promising but belief is unstable
- `low resonance` - the project does not currently create enough belief
- `unclear signal` - the project may be too early or too underexplained to judge

## Publication rule

Named-project resonance reviews should be published only when:

- the owner agrees to public publication, or
- the review uses only public materials, avoids private conversation details, and stays fair, or
- the project is anonymized into a reusable pattern.

Private feelings can be honest. Public writing must be ethical.
