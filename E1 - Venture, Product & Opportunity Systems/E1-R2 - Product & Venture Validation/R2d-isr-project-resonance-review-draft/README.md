# R2d ISR Project Resonance Review Draft

## Status

Consent-required draft.

This is a public-safe placeholder for a future resonance review of ISR - Index of Social Responsibility.

It is separate from the ISR project roast. The roast should diagnose blockers and recommend improvements. This resonance review should capture personal perception, associations, belief, doubts, and whether the project feels promising as a social, civic, or venture-facing idea.

## Project

- Project: ISR - Index of Social Responsibility
- Public site: https://isr-vote.com/
- Review type: project resonance review
- Method: `R2c Project Resonance Review Methodology`

## Why this case belongs in E1-R2

ISR is not only a product-positioning problem.

It also creates a strong perspective question:

```text
Do I personally believe this project has a future, and what kind of future do I see?
```

That question is different from:

```text
What should the founder improve next?
```

## Draft review angle

The future ISR resonance review can separate:

- what feels intellectually interesting
- what feels socially meaningful
- what feels confusing or risky
- what associations appear around governance, voting, public trust, social scoring, DAOs, civic tech, and reputation systems
- whether the project feels like a startup, research artifact, civic infrastructure experiment, community tool, or public-intellectual concept
- whether the reviewer personally sees perspective and why

## Suggested structure for the ISR resonance review

1. Project snapshot
2. First emotional/intellectual impression
3. What feels compelling
4. What feels uncomfortable or weak
5. Association map
6. Perspective verdict
7. Future paths I can imagine
8. What I would keep watching
9. Public-value meaning
10. Final note

## Boundary from the ISR roast

The ISR roast may include:

- cold-user readiness score
- commercial readiness score
- adoption blockers
- landing-page critique
- pilot and positioning recommendations
- 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day action plan

The ISR resonance review should avoid turning into a task list. It may be critical, even strongly critical, but its job is to record argued perspective rather than deliver founder instructions.

## Publication rule

Do not publish a named, detailed ISR resonance review based on private conversations unless the project owner explicitly approves it.

A public-source-only version can be written from the project website and public channels, but it should clearly avoid implying access to private founder context.
